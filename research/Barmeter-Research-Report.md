## 1. Executive Summary

**Barmeter Technologies** is a young (founded 2021), services-led firm in the **AEC (Architecture/Engineering/Construction)** supply chain. They specialize in **structural steel detailing and connection engineering** for U.S. fabricators, general contractors, and architects.

Their value proposition is "**fabrication-first**, **sequence-ready** detailing" — drawings ordered to match the way steel will actually be fabricated and erected, reducing RFIs and field rework.

The company runs a classic **onshore-sales / offshore-production** model: U.S. presence in Texas, Oklahoma, and California for client work and P.E. licensure (claimed 39+ states / ~80% U.S. coverage); production teams in **Hyderabad and Bengaluru, India**. The Bengaluru facility doubles as an **authorized SDS/2 (Nemetschek) training center** — training serves both as a small revenue line and as a recruiting pipeline for their own production team.

The vision they articulate publicly is bigger than services: a "unified intelligence platform" for steel construction. That suggests an eventual move from pure services into productized workflow tools (the implied long-term play).

## 3. What They Do (Plain English)

When a building or industrial structure is going to have a **steel skeleton**, somebody has to translate the structural engineer's design drawings into precise, fabrication-ready shop drawings — every beam, column, plate, bolt, weld, and how each joint connects.

**Barmeter is that translator** between design intent and the steel fabricator/erector on site.

Their differentiator is **sequence**: drawings are organized the way the steel will actually be fabricated in the shop and erected on site, instead of the way it appears in the design model. That ordering is what they claim cuts RFIs (requests for information) and rework.

```
    Architect / Structural Engineer  (design intent)
                    │
                    ▼
        ┌────────────────────────────┐
        │       BARMETER             │
        │  detailing · connections   │
        │  RFIs · takeoffs · BIM     │
        └────────────────────────────┘
                    │
                    ▼
   Steel Fabricator → General Contractor → Erector (field)
```

## 8. Business Model

### Two revenue arms

#### 1. Services (the main business)
Sells steel detailing, connection design, estimating, and erection support. Customers are primarily:
- **Steel fabricators** — the most common buyer of detailing services
- **General contractors** — when GCs procure detailing directly
- **Structural engineers / architects** — less common, usually routed via the GC or fabricator

Pricing is project-based on tonnage, complexity, schedule, and connection scope.

#### 2. Training (the SDS/2 academy)
Authorized Nemetschek training center in Bengaluru. Trains detailers on SDS/2.

The strategic value extends beyond direct revenue: it functions as a **recruiting funnel**. Top students get hired into the production team, lowering the cost of acquiring trained labor.

### Onshore / offshore split

| Side | Function |
|---|---|
| **U.S.** | Sales, P.E. licensure (39+ states), client communication, code interpretation, on-site support |
| **India** (Hyd / Blr) | Production: modelers, checkers; SDS/2 training |

This is the standard structure for competitive AEC services firms — keeps cost-per-hour low while staying close to American clients and AISC standards.

### Differentiation claims
- **"Sequence-ready"** detailing → fewer RFIs, less field rework
- **Zero-rework philosophy** with structured QC
- **80% U.S. coverage** on connection P.E. stamping
- **Tech-driven workflow** — automation in detailing
- **"Project partner"** positioning — not just a vendor

### Where they sit in the value chain

```
Architect / Structural Engineer (design intent)
              │
              ▼
   ┌───────────────────────┐
   │      BARMETER         │  ← detailing, connection design,
   │     (translation)     │     RFIs, takeoffs, coordination
   └───────────────────────┘
              │
              ▼
Steel Fabricator (shop) → General Contractor → Erector (field)
```

### Implied long-term play
The vision language ("unified intelligence platform," "one seamless organism") strongly suggests a roadmap toward **vertical SaaS** — productizing the detailing/RFI/sequence workflow. That's where margin sits. Services scale with headcount; software scales without it.

## 10. Competitive Landscape

Barmeter operates in a fragmented global market. Rough segmentation:

### Direct / nearest competitors
- **Tekla / SDS/2 service shops in India and the Philippines** — broad pool of low-cost detailing teams
- **Established U.S. detailing firms** — Dowco, Eastern Steel Constructors' detailing arm, Steele Solutions
- **Independent / fabricator-affiliated detailing teams**

### Adjacent / threatening
- **In-house detailing at large fabricators** — some large U.S. fabricators have their own detailing groups
- **Software vendors moving downstream** — Tekla, SDS/2 themselves expanding consulting/services
- **AI / automation** — emerging tools that auto-generate connections, drawings, and BOMs (medium-term threat to low-end detailing labor)

### How a young firm like Barmeter wins
- **Reliability + speed** — services market is reputation-driven
- **U.S. P.E. coverage** — the 39+ state P.E. claim is a real moat for connection design work
- **Vertical integration** of detailing, connection design, estimating, BIM, training
- **Eventually:** productized workflow → defensible IP

## 11. Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Risks

### Strengths
- Broad service portfolio (one-stop shop for fabricators)
- Wide U.S. P.E. coverage for stamped connection work
- Authorized SDS/2 training partnership = recruiting moat
- Onshore/offshore cost structure
- Clear, modern brand and positioning ("fabrication-first, sequence-ready")

### Weaknesses
- Founded 2021 — limited public reputation/track record vs. incumbents
- Leadership not named publicly — reduces trust signal for large GCs
- No published case studies with metrics (tonnage, schedule wins, $ saved)
- Pricing opacity is industry-standard but limits inbound conversion
- Concentration risk on Texas market

### Opportunities
- **Productize the workflow** — RFI tracker, sequence visualizer, connection-design assistant → SaaS
- **Expand into Mountain West / Southeast** where construction backlogs are growing
- **AI-augmented detailing** — own the automation curve instead of being disrupted by it
- **Training revenue scale-up** — the SDS/2 program could become a meaningful standalone P&L

### Risks
- **Construction cyclicality** — services revenue tied to U.S. nonresidential building cycle
- **AI commoditization** of low-end detailing labor over a 3–5 year horizon
- **P.E. licensure dependency** — if key engineers leave, state coverage shrinks
- **Currency / wage inflation in India** compressing the offshore margin advantage over time
- **Reputation risk** from a single bad project (services word-of-mouth)
