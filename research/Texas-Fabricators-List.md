# Texas Steel Fabricators — Target List

> Curated starting list of Texas steel fabricators and key GCs to seed your outbound pipeline. Treat this as the **first 25 of 50** — extend it from the AISC directory before you start outreach.
>
> Compiled 2026-05-09 from public sources. **Verify all phone/email/website data before contacting** — this changes frequently.

---

## How to use this list

1. **Triage by sub-market** (Houston, DFW, Austin/Central, Waco corridor) — pick the one or two closest to you.
2. **Find the right person** — owner / president / VP operations / chief estimator / detailing manager. Use LinkedIn for names.
3. **Tier them** — Tier 1 = AISC certified + your ICP; Tier 2 = mid-size non-certified; Tier 3 = research more.
4. **Track in a CRM** — even a Google Sheet works. Columns: Company · Sub-market · Tier · Decision-maker · LinkedIn URL · Last contacted · Status · Next step.

## How to extend this list to 50+

### Free directories
- **AISC "Find a Fabricator"** → https://www.aisc.org/membership/finding-a-member/find-a-fabricator/ → Filter: State = Texas, Categories = Building / Bridge / Erector / Service Center, Certifications = applicable
- **Houston Permitting Center — Registered Fabricators List** → https://www.houstonpermittingcenter.org/media/2146/download (downloadable PDF of Houston-area certified fabs)
- **The Blue Book Network — Texas** → https://www.thebluebook.com/
- **Procore Network** → https://www.procore.com/network/us/tx/structural-steel
- **Dun & Bradstreet — Texas Architectural & Structural Metals Manufacturing** → https://www.dnb.com/business-directory/company-information.architectural_and_structural_metals_manufacturing.us.texas.html
- **IQS Directory — Texas Metal Fabrication** → https://www.iqsdirectory.com/steel-fabricators/texas/

### Paid (better for serious outreach)
- **Dodge Construction Network** — bid pipeline + project leads
- **Construction Connect (iSqFt)** — bidding platform; you can see who's bidding what
- **BuildingConnected** — used by big GCs for bid invites
- **ZoomInfo / Apollo.io** — pull contact data (chief estimator, VP ops emails) for any of the above companies

### LinkedIn search strategies
Search:
- "Chief Estimator" AND "structural steel" AND "Texas"
- "VP Operations" AND "fabricator" AND "Texas"
- "Detailing Manager" AND ("Dallas" OR "Houston" OR "Austin")
- "President" AND "Steel" AND "Texas"

Sales Navigator subscription ($99/mo) speeds this up dramatically — worth the cost for 6 months of focused prospecting.

## Decision-makers to find for each fabricator

In priority order:
1. **President / Owner** — for shops <$25M revenue, this is your buyer
2. **VP Operations / GM** — for shops $25M–$100M
3. **Chief Estimator / Estimating Manager** — they often initiate outsourced detailing decisions
4. **Project Manager** — operational gatekeeper, useful for in
5. **Detailing Manager / Detailing Coordinator** — if it exists, this is the person who chooses subs

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Don't blast a generic mass-email — Texas industry is small, word travels
- ❌ Don't lead with price — lead with reliability and a metric
- ❌ Don't try to sell to all 50 in week 1 — pick 10, work them deep
- ❌ Don't ignore the Houston Permitting Center registered list — those are pre-qualified buyers
- ❌ Don't skip industry events — ONE NASCC trip is worth 100 cold emails
