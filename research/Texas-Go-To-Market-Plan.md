# Texas Go-To-Market Plan — Steel Detailing Services

> A concrete, executable GTM for landing your first 5–10 paying fabricator clients in Texas within 12 months.
>
> Compiled 2026-05-09. See [Texas-Fabricators-List.md](Texas-Fabricators-List.md) for the target account list.

---

## Why Texas is the right beachhead in 2026

- **Texas GCs reported $127B+ in contracting revenue last year, +17.5% YoY.** Nonresidential building starts grew ~13% in 2025, with continued growth expected in 2026.
- **Megaproject pipeline is loaded:** semiconductor fabs (Samsung Taylor, TI Sherman, Micron), data centers across DFW/Austin/West Texas, biomanufacturing on the Gulf Coast, JCB's $500M San Antonio plant (5K+ tons of steel erection through 2026).
- **Steel-heavy verticals are growing fastest:** education, healthcare, industrial, and data centers — all big steel consumers.
- **Concentration of AISC-certified fabricators** in Houston, DFW, Austin/San Antonio, and Waco corridor — buyers are geographically clustered.
- **Reshoring + Dallas Fed industrial-boom data** confirms Texas leads U.S. industrial construction growth.

**Implication:** This is a buyer's market for capacity-constrained fabricators looking for reliable detailing partners.

## The 4 Texas sub-markets to focus on

Geographic targeting matters — drive distance and shop relationships are everything in this business.

| Sub-market | Why it matters | Anchor cities |
|---|---|---|
| **Houston / Gulf Coast** | Industrial, energy, petrochemical, healthcare. Largest concentration of fabricators. Mobil Steel, Sefton Steel, Associated Steel Fab here. | Houston, Beaumont, Pasadena |
| **DFW Metroplex** | Commercial + warehouse + healthcare + data center. Lexicon (Dallas), distribution centers, hospitals. | Dallas, Fort Worth, Plano, Arlington |
| **Austin / Central Texas / I-35 corridor** | Tech, semiconductor (Samsung Taylor, TI Sherman), education (UT, A&M), municipal. Patriot Erectors anchor here. | Austin, Round Rock, San Antonio, Waco |
| **Waco / Hamilton corridor** | Mid-Texas industrial spine. Central Texas Iron Works (Waco), C&F Steel (Hamilton). High-volume industrial fab. | Waco, Hamilton, Temple |

**Recommendation:** Pick **two sub-markets** for your first 12 months. Houston + DFW gives you the largest TAM. Austin + Waco gives you less competition and a cleaner story.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) for Year 1

Don't try to win every fab. Focus.

**Tier 1 — Best Fit (target first):**
- AISC-certified fabricator
- 200–2,000 tons/month production
- Location in your chosen sub-market
- Project mix: commercial, healthcare, education, light industrial
- Pain signal: hiring "estimator" or "detailing coordinator" or has cyclical detailing peaks
- Annual revenue: $10M–$75M (big enough to need outside detailing, small enough that you can reach the decision-maker in 1–2 calls)

**Tier 2 — Acceptable:**
- Non-certified mid-size shops doing strip mall, warehouse, light commercial
- Faster sales cycles, lower-margin work — good for filling the schedule

**Avoid in Year 1:**
- Bridge fabricators (separate certification, separate skill — NSBA)
- Bottom-tier price shoppers (will switch over $5/ton)
- The biggest fabricators (Lexicon, Patriot) — they have entrenched detailers; come back in year 2

## Positioning — How to differentiate in Texas

Texas fabricators have heard every pitch. Pick **one** sharp angle:

1. **"Sequence-ready, RFI-killer detailing"** — the Barmeter angle. Lead with a metric (e.g., "<2 RFIs per 100 tons on our last 10 jobs").
2. **"Texas-licensed P.E. on every connection"** — if you have a P.E. with TX registration, this is huge. Most offshore competitors don't.
3. **"24-hour RFI turnaround, no exceptions"** — operational SLA. Easy to advertise, easy to keep, easy to differentiate.
4. **"Healthcare specialist"** — narrow vertical positioning if you have hospital experience. Texas hospital pipeline is huge through 2026.
5. **"Semiconductor / cleanroom steel"** — niche, high-margin, growing fast (Samsung, TI, Micron).

**Don't lead with:** "We use SDS/2 and Tekla." Everyone does. It's table stakes, not differentiation.

## 90-day execution plan

### Days 1–14 — Foundation
- [ ] Finalize entity, insurance ($1M E&O minimum), business bank account
- [ ] Confirm software stack (SDS/2 OR Tekla — pick one for year 1)
- [ ] Sign sub-contract with a Texas-licensed P.E. for connection stamping (per-job, not employed)
- [ ] One-page website live with 3 services, contact form, your phone number
- [ ] LinkedIn profile + company page set up
- [ ] Build target list of 50 Texas fabricators (use the [list](Texas-Fabricators-List.md) as starting point + AISC's "Find a Fabricator" search filtered to Texas)

### Days 15–30 — Asset creation
- [ ] One sample case study (1 page PDF): project type, tonnage, schedule, drawings count, RFI count
- [ ] One downloadable "Connection Detailing Capabilities" sheet
- [ ] 3 LinkedIn posts: introduction, a project insight, a technical tip

### Days 31–60 — Outreach wave 1
- [ ] LinkedIn-connect every target's owner / chief estimator / VP ops (target: 50 connections)
- [ ] Send 50 personalized intro emails (template in [How-to-Start-Steel-Detailing-Business.md §9](How-to-Start-Steel-Detailing-Business.md#9-sales-playbook--finding--winning-clients))
- [ ] Show up at the next monthly Houston AGC chapter meeting (or Dallas-area equivalent)
- [ ] Follow-up call to anyone who replied

### Days 61–90 — Convert + double-down on what's working
- [ ] Aim for 3–5 first paid pilots (small packages, $5K–$15K each — earn the trust)
- [ ] Track every interaction in a simple CRM (HubSpot Free, Notion, or even a Google Sheet)
- [ ] Identify which sub-market and which ICP is responding — kill what's not working
- [ ] Book travel + booth visit to **NASCC: The Steel Conference** (next session — usually March/April; the single highest-leverage event)

## Texas-specific channels & places to be

### Industry events (in-state)
- **AISC SteelDay Texas events** — September each year, hosted by local fab/erector associations
- **Houston AGC chapter meetings** — monthly, easy access
- **TEXO (Dallas/Fort Worth chapter of ABC + AGC)** events
- **Texas Society of Architects annual convention** — tangential but useful for cross-pollination
- **Texas A&M Construction Career Fair** — recruiting + relationships with future GC PMs

### National events Texas fabricators attend
- **NASCC: The Steel Conference** — annual, AISC-run. Mandatory.
- **AISC Education Foundation events** in Texas universities (UT-Austin, Texas A&M, Texas Tech)

### Online
- **AISC Find a Fabricator** — filter by Texas, category (Building, Bridge), certification
- **The Blue Book Network** — Texas chapter is heavily used by GCs and subs
- **Procore Network** — many Texas GCs use this
- **Dodge Construction Network / iSqFt** — bid pipelines (paid)
- **LinkedIn** — your single most important channel for personal selling

### Publications to read & post in
- **Modern Steel Construction** (AISC's magazine) — ad placement starts ~$3K/issue, but a **technical article** is free PR
- **Texas Construction News** — regional trade publication
- **STRUCTURE Magazine** — engineer audience, useful for connection design positioning

## Pricing strategy for Texas Year 1

- **Entry price:** $90–$110 / ton for standard commercial detailing (sits at the middle of Texas market)
- **Premium price:** $150–$200 / ton for healthcare, education, semiconductor cleanroom steel
- **Connection design (with TX P.E. stamp):** quote separately at 8–12% of detailing fee, or hourly at $150–$200/hr
- **Project minimum:** $7,500 (don't take anything smaller in year 1 — it eats time)
- **First-customer discount:** offer a 10% discount on first project (capped at $5K) in exchange for a written reference and case study rights

## What success looks like at Day 365

- 4–6 active fabricator clients with repeat work
- $30–50K MRR in committed/recurring detailing
- 3 written case studies (with metrics)
- NISD Individual Detailer Certification in progress or complete
- AISC-conforming alignment for your QC processes
- One vertical you're known for (e.g., "the healthcare detailers in Houston")
- Booth or attendee at NASCC, with 20+ new contacts

If you're not at this point in 12 months, the issue is likely either (a) wrong sub-market, (b) too generic a pitch, or (c) not enough outbound volume — not the market itself.
